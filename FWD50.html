DESIGNING A USER CENTRIC DIGITAL CHANNEL FOR SERVICE CANADA

Although there is an excellent all-of-Canadian Government communications channel in Canada.ca, there is not a Service Canada experience online that provides anywhere near the level of user centricity or quality of client/customer service that our non-digital channels offer. People who want to self-serve online need an end to end, integrated and supported experience. Our team is taking a user-centric and design led approach to designing a Digital Channel for Service Canada. Our goal is to create a dignified, user-centric, responsive and integrated digital journey that delights, empowers and supports Canadians to get the government services and benefits you need and are entitled to. The Digital Channel will also include an operating model that supports users within channel, continuously innovates as part of BAU, and assumes constant change and responsiveness to new needs and challenges. We want to do the hard work to make it easy for Canadians, which means a shift away from the current state where the onus generally on end users to navigate the impossible landscape of ever-changing and necessarily siloed bureaucracy. We want your help to shape a digital channel for Service Canada, so it can be developed with the very people it needs to serve. Pia Andrews Open and digital government expert, Special Advisor at Service Canada

DIGITAL TRANSFORMATION AND ENVIRONMENTAL GOVERNANCE

Digital tech has an ambivalent relationship with the environment. The tech sector has recently begun to focus on the challenge of reducing greenhouse gas emissions as digital transformation accelerates, via the greening of IT and procurement. Beyond these critical issues loom larger challenges and opportunities. In this session, we explore one critical challenge: public governance of environmental data is not keeping pace with fast-paced private sector innovation in instrumentation and environmental data harvesting, raising unprecedented policy challenges for governments around the world. We also explore a promising opportunity: digital tech innovations are exponentially accelerating government's ability to engage in environmental monitoring, protection, and enforcement. These developments sit at the confluence of Canada's Innovation and Skills agenda, and its domestic and international environmental stewardship responsibilities--creating an exciting opportunity for driving innovation and renewing talent in the public sector and beyond. David Jensen Head of the Digital Transformation Task Force, UN Environment Program Dennis Skinner Executive Director, Digital Change Sector, Office of the CIO of Canada, Treasury Board of Canada Secretariat Dr. Karen Bakker Professor, Canada Research Chair and Co-Director, Program on Water Governance, University of British Columbia
